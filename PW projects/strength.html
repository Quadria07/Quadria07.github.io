What is a password strength tester?

A password strength tester gauges how long it might hypothetically take to crack your password by testing the password against a set of known criteria–such as length, randomness, and complexity. Using a password strength tester is an easy step to securing your online profile. In seconds, they help you determine whether or not your passwords are strong enough to protect your online subscriptions, such as your bank account.

  • Length: Longer passwords are generally more secure.
  • Character Complexity: The use of a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
  • Dictionary Words: Dictionary Words: Avoiding the use of easily guessable words found in dictionaries.
  • Patterns and Repetitions: Avoiding repetitive sequences or easily guessable patterns.
  • Unpredictability: Ensuring the password is not easily guessable based on personal information or common patterns.
How to Set Up Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for Your Social Media Account

Certainly! Below is a basic guide on how to set up multi-factor authentication (MFA) and two-factor authentication (2FA) for a social media account. Note that the steps might vary slightly depending on the platform, but this provides a general overview:


  • Step 1: Log into Your Account

    Visit the Platform:

    Go to the official website or app of the social media platform you want to secure.

    Login:

    Enter your username and password to log into your account.


  • Step 2: Access Security Settings

    Navigate to Account Settings:

    Look for an option like "Account Settings," "Security," or "Privacy".

    Locate Two-Factor Authentication:

    Find the section related to two-factor or multi-factor authentication.


  • Step 3: Enable Two-Factor Authentication

    Choose a Method:

    Select the type of 2FA you want to use. Common methods include:

  • SMS Code: Receive a code via text message.
  • Authentication App: Use a time-based one-time password (TOTP) app like Google Authenticator or Authy.

    • Enter Phone Number (if applicable):

    If using SMS, provide your phone number. You'll receive a code each time you log in.


  • Step 4: Save Backup Codes

    Generate Backup Codes:

    Some platforms provide backup codes. Generate and save them in a secure place.

    Use Backup Codes Wisely:

    Only use backup codes if you're unable to access your primary authentication method.


  • Step 5: Confirm Two-Factor Authentication

    Test the Setup:

    Log out and log back in to confirm that two-factor authentication is working.


  • Step 6: Additional Security Tips

    Keep Backup Codes Safe:

    Store backup codes securely, preferably offline.

    Update Recovery Information:

    Ensure your account recovery information is up-to-date.

    Use App-Based Authentication:

    Whenever possible, use authentication apps rather than SMS for increased security.

By enabling two-factor authentication, you've significantly enhanced the security of your social media account. Regularly review your security settings and stay informed about new features to keep your account safe.
